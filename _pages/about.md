Felipe Feijoo is an Associate Professor at the [School of Industrial Engineering](http://eii.pucv.cl/), Pontificia Universidad Catolica de Valparaiso (PUCV), Chile. His research sits at the intersection of energy systems modeling, climate policy, and operations research, with emphasis on the design of deep decarbonization pathways under uncertainty and the interactions between the energy, water, land, and food systems.

He currently serves as a **Lead Author for the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC), Working Group III – Energy Systems**, as an **Advisor to the Scientific Committee on Climate Change at the Ministry of Environment of Chile**, and as a **Consultant for APEC** through the Ministry of Energy of Chile on hydrogen certification policies across Asia-Pacific economies.

Research areas
======
* Long-term energy and climate policy modeling (energy transitions, net-zero and net-negative pathways)
* Integrated assessment and multi-scale energy planning (global, regional, and local)
* Power-to-X, hydrogen, and flexibility technologies
* Stochastic optimization, bilevel and Stackelberg games applied to electricity markets and microgrids
* Energy-water-food nexus and just transitions
* AI-aided energy planning and decarbonization analytics

Current and recent projects
======
* **DECARB-R+J** (FONDECYT Regular, 2026-2029, PI): Advancing deep decarbonization pathways through endogenous modeling of resilience, justice, and renewable integration.
* **APEC Hydrogen Certification** (APEC EWG 104-105, 2025, PI): Building resilient energy policies in Asia-Pacific &mdash; certification of hydrogen and derivatives in APEC economies.
* **EquiEnergy** (ANID FOVI, 2024, PI): Empowering just transitions for energy efficiency and decarbonization.
* **INTERNEXUS** (FONDECYT Regular, 2022, PI): Interrelation between the energy-water-food nexus and Power-to-X for deep decarbonization pathways toward 100% renewable energy systems.
* **HEAT** (DI Centenario PUCV, 2024, PI): Health, Energy, and Transportation AI-aided for smart cities.
